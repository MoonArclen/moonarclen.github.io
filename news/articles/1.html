The Story Behind My Ban From a Community I Once Called Home

Published by Moon Arclen • Community Report

I was one of Mellow’s earliest followers on both Twitch and YouTube. Back then, the streams were more than content to me — they were a place I genuinely enjoyed being part of.

One day, my YouTube account was compromised. While the hacker had access, my account was used to spam messages in live chat reading #JoinTheBots. Around that same time, several bot accounts began appearing using similar messages.

Because of what happened, I was banned. After explaining that my account had been hacked, I was later unbanned. I secured my account by enabling two-factor authentication and believed everything had been resolved.

Months later, another account appeared under the name @nxtmellowajc. People claimed it was an alternate account created by Mellow. At some point, someone close to Mellow stated that the account had been created by me.

I never created that account. Yet the accusation spread. When Mellow returned to Twitch for one of the few streams after moving mostly to YouTube, I joined simply to say hello and ask why I appeared banned.

Her response in stream was direct: I was told I was not welcome and accused of creating alternate accounts and impersonating her. Moments later, I was banned again.

Since then, whenever I appear in a stream where someone close to Mellow is present or moderating, I am removed. It feels as though my name alone is enough to trigger action.

I am sharing this because all I want is a fair review of what happened. I supported that community from the beginning, and despite everything, all I want is to return to what was once my favorite streamer’s community.
